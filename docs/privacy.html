Privacy Model

Privacy by Mathematics

Resonance doesn't protect your privacy with a policy document. It protects it with math, cryptography, and architecture decisions that make surveillance structurally impossible.

Foundation

Three Independent Layers

Each layer operates independently. Even if one is compromised, the others still protect you. This is defense in depth — not redundancy, but independent guarantees.

🧠
Layer 1

Local AI

The embedding model (Nomic-embed-text) runs entirely on your device. Your text is converted to a 768-dimensional vector locally using ONNX runtime. No cloud API call, no server-side processing, no third-party service. The raw text never touches a network interface.

🛡
Layer 2

Differential Privacy

Calibrated Laplace noise is added to every dimension of every vector before transmission. This provides formal ε-differential privacy guarantees. Even with unlimited computation, the original text cannot be reconstructed from the noisy representation.

🔒
Layer 3

E2E Encryption

Direct channels between matched parties use X25519 Diffie-Hellman key exchange to derive a shared secret, then XSalsa20-Poly1305 authenticated encryption for all messages. The relay cannot read channel contents. Only you and your match hold the keys.

Independence matters

If a future vulnerability is found in the embedding model, your data is still protected by differential privacy noise and encryption. If the encryption library has a bug, your raw text was never sent anyway. If the noise calibration is weakened, the relay still only sees vectors — never text, never identity. Each layer stands alone.

Differential Privacy

Privacy Levels

You choose how much noise is added to your vector. The trade-off is yours to make: more noise means stronger privacy but fewer matches. The formula is transparent and auditable.

Structural Constraints

What the Relay Cannot Do

The relay operator — whether that's us, a company, or a community — is structurally prevented from performing these actions. Not by policy. By mathematics and architecture.

🚫

Cannot Read Your Text

Your text is embedded locally and never transmitted. The relay receives only a noisy 768-dimensional vector — a mathematical shadow that cannot be inverted back to text.

🚫

Cannot See Your True Embedding

Differential privacy noise is added before transmission. The relay operates on perturbed vectors. The true embedding exists only on your device.

🚫

Cannot Decrypt Channel Messages

Channels use end-to-end encryption with keys derived from a Diffie-Hellman exchange between peers. The relay transports ciphertext it cannot read.

🚫

Cannot Link You to Your Real Identity

Your identity is a DID (did:key) generated from an Ed25519 keypair created offline. No email, no phone, no social login. The DID is pseudonymous by construction.

🚫

Cannot Block Progressive Disclosure

Progressive disclosure is controlled by your node, not the relay. The relay has no mechanism to force, prevent, or observe what you share in encrypted channels.

🚫

Cannot See What You Share in Channels

All channel content — categories, details, contact info — is encrypted end-to-end. The relay is a blind courier that routes sealed envelopes.

Not promises. Mathematical constraints.

These aren't pledges in a terms-of-service document. They are consequences of the system's architecture. Even a malicious relay operator — one who wants to violate your privacy — cannot do so. The data they would need simply does not exist on their infrastructure. A subpoena to the relay yields perturbed vectors and pseudonymous DIDs: no text, no names, no contacts, no messages.

Identity

Decentralized Identifiers (DIDs)

Your identity in Resonance is a cryptographic keypair, not an account on someone else's server. No registration. No email verification. No phone number. No passwords.

Resonance uses the did:key method with Ed25519 keypairs. Your identity is generated entirely offline in a single cryptographic operation. The resulting DID is a globally unique, self-certifying identifier that proves you are you without revealing who you are.

// Your identity is a DID generated from an Ed25519 keypair did:key:z6MkhaXgBZDvotDkL5257faiztiGiC2QtKLGpbnnEGta2doK // No email, no phone, no social login, no password // Created offline, instantly, for free // Your private key never leaves your device
🔑

Offline Creation

Generate a new identity with no internet connection. It's a cryptographic operation, not a server request.

🔀

Multiple Identities

Create as many DIDs as you need. Each is mathematically independent — no one can correlate them unless you choose to reveal the connection.

📦

Export & Import

Back up your identity with password-encrypted export files. Restore on any device. Your keys, your control.

🔐

Encrypted at Rest

Private keys are stored in a local encrypted database, protected by a passphrase you set during initialization.

Information Control

Progressive Disclosure

Information sharing happens in stages. Each stage requires your explicit, deliberate action. Nothing is shared automatically. The protocol defaults to silence.

01
Match Notification
Similarity score + DID only. You don't know who they are or what they wrote.
→
02
Category
"Backend development" — high-level topic, no specifics.
→
03
Detail
"Django expert, available remote" — specific but still pseudonymous.
→
04
Contact
Email, phone, website — only if you choose.

Each step is gated by mutual consent. Both parties must agree to advance to the next disclosure level. If you decide at any point that the match isn't right, you simply stop. No data has been leaked, no profile has been built, no trace remains on the relay.

Design philosophy

You control every step. The protocol cannot force disclosure. Information flows outward only when you push it — the system never pulls it from you. This is the opposite of how traditional platforms work, where sharing is the default and privacy requires opt-out.

Architecture

Trust Boundaries

Different components of the system have access to different data. Here is exactly what each component can and cannot see.

Data Personal Node Relay Direct Channel
Original text Yes No No

Perturbed embedding Yes Yes No
DID identity Yes Yes (pseudonymous) Yes (pseudonymous)
Real identity Yes No Only if disclosed

Disclosure content Yes No Yes (as shared)
GDPR by Architecture

Because the relay holds no personal data — only perturbed vectors and pseudonymous DIDs — there is no personal data to regulate, delete, or breach. This isn't GDPR compliance through legal effort; it's GDPR irrelevance through architectural design. There is nothing to subpoena because there is nothing to store.
