Privacy

Your data never leaves your device.

Planetary Resonance is built on the principle that a matching network should never need to know what it's matching. Three independent layers of privacy guarantee this isn't a promise — it's a mathematical fact.

Three Layers of Privacy

Each layer operates independently. Even if one is compromised, the others still protect you.

🧠

Local AI

The embedding model runs entirely on your machine. Your text is converted to a 768-dimensional vector locally. No cloud API call, no server-side processing, no third-party service. The raw text never touches a network interface.

nomic-embed-text v1.5 · ~137 MB · ONNX runtime
🛡

Differential Privacy

Calibrated Laplace noise is added to every dimension of every vector before transmission. This provides formal ε-differential privacy guarantees. Even if a vector is intercepted, the original text cannot be reconstructed from the noisy representation.

Laplace mechanism · ε ∈ {0.1, 1.0, 5.0} · per-dimension noise
🔒

End-to-End Encryption

Direct channels between matched parties use X25519 Diffie-Hellman key exchange with XSalsa20-Poly1305 authenticated encryption. The relay never sees channel contents. Only you and your match can read messages.

X25519 + XSalsa20-Poly1305 · libsodium · forward secrecy

Privacy Levels

You choose how much noise is added to your vector. Higher epsilon means less noise and better matching. Lower epsilon means more noise and stronger privacy. The trade-off is yours to make.

Level Epsilon (ε) Similarity to Original Recall Best For
Low 5.0 99.4% 98% Maximum matching accuracy. Use when content sensitivity is low.
Medium 1.0 88.1% 82–86% Balanced privacy and utility. The default setting for most users.
High 0.1 18.2% ~14% Maximum privacy, significantly fewer matches. For highly sensitive needs.

DID Identity

Your identity in Planetary Resonance is a cryptographic keypair (Ed25519), expressed as a Decentralized Identifier (DID). Not an email address. Not a phone number. Not a username tied to a corporation's database.

did:key:z6MkhaXgBZDvotDkL5257faiztiGiC2QtKLGpbnnEGta2doK

You can create new identities at any time, for free, instantly, offline. There is no "account" to create, no registration flow, no email verification. Each identity is mathematically independent — no one can correlate your different DIDs unless you choose to reveal the connection.

Your private key never leaves your device. It's stored in a local encrypted database, protected by a passphrase you set during initialization. Even if someone gains access to your machine, the key is encrypted at rest.

Progressive Disclosure

Information sharing happens in stages. Each stage requires your explicit, deliberate action. Nothing is shared automatically.

1. Match Notification

You receive a similarity score and the match type (need/offer). Nothing else. You don't know who they are, what they wrote, or where they are. Just: "someone out there is a potential match at 87% similarity."

2. Category-Level Details

If both parties consent, you exchange high-level category information. "This is about software development" or "This is about home repair." Still no specifics, no names, no contact info.

3. Specific Details

With continued mutual consent, you share the specific details of your need or offer. "I'm looking for a React developer for a 3-month dashboard project." The other party shares theirs. You evaluate fit.

4. Contact Information

Only when both parties are satisfied that the match is genuine and valuable, you exchange contact information. Email, phone, website — whatever you choose. This is the final step, and it's entirely optional.

What we can't do

The relay operator — whether that's us, a company, or a community — cannot:

  • Read your original text
  • See your name or real identity
  • Know your physical location
  • Track your activity across sessions (different DIDs are unlinkable)
  • Sell your data (because they don't have it)
  • Comply with a data request (because there is no data to hand over)
  • Build a profile of your interests over time
  • Reconstruct your original embedding from the noisy version

This isn't a privacy policy. It's a technical constraint. The system is designed so that these capabilities are mathematically impossible, not merely prohibited by policy.
