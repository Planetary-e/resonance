About

What Is Planetary Resonance?

Planetary Resonance is an open-source, privacy-preserving protocol for matching people with complementary needs and offers. It uses on-device AI embedding, differential privacy, and end-to-end encryption to enable peer-to-peer discovery without any central platform seeing user data. A person describes what they need or what they offer in plain language; the protocol converts this to a mathematical vector on the user's device, adds calibrated noise for privacy protection, and publishes only the protected vector to a decentralized relay mesh. When two users have complementary intent above a similarity threshold, they're matched and can communicate through an encrypted channel — revealing details progressively and on their own terms.

Differentiators

What Makes It Different

Five architectural decisions that distinguish Resonance from every matching platform that exists today.

🧮

Mathematical Privacy, Not Policy Privacy

Most platforms protect your data with a privacy policy — a document that can change. Resonance uses differential privacy (Laplace mechanism) to ensure that no entity in the network can reconstruct your original input. This is a mathematical guarantee that holds regardless of who operates the relay.

🔄

Complementary, Not Duplicate Matching

Search engines and social platforms match similar content to similar content. Resonance matches needs to offers — it understands that "I need a plumber" and "I do plumbing" are complementary, not duplicates. This is the fundamental insight that makes the protocol useful for real-world coordination.

🚫

No Accounts, No Profiles

Identity is a locally-generated cryptographic keypair (did:key with Ed25519). No email, no phone number, no social login, no password, no username. Users exist as keys, not as profiles in a database.

📱

Consumer Hardware Only

The entire protocol — including AI embedding — runs on consumer hardware at 14ms p95 on CPU. No GPU, no cloud API, no external service dependency. A laptop, a Raspberry Pi, or a phone is enough.

🔎

Fully Auditable

Every line of code is open source under the MIT license. The privacy guarantees are not claims — they are verifiable properties of the code. 119 tests and 35 evaluation metrics ensure the system behaves as documented.

For Your Coverage

Quotes & Talking Points

The following statements can be attributed to the project. Use them freely in your coverage.

On the Problem

"Every matching platform today — job boards, housing sites, dating apps, freelance marketplaces — requires you to hand all your data to a middleman. Resonance proves that the middleman was never necessary for the matching itself. The math works without it."

On Privacy

"We don't protect your privacy with a policy document. We protect it with Laplace noise, differential privacy, and end-to-end encryption. The relay literally cannot read what you said — not because we promise not to, but because the information isn't there."

On Performance

"Privacy has a cost, and we measured it precisely: at medium privacy (ε=1.0), you keep 82-86% of match recall with a false positive rate under 3%. The embedding takes 14 milliseconds on a CPU. That's the trade-off, and we think it's worth it."

On the Vision

"Resonance is infrastructure for a world where people can find each other without being watched. It's not an app — it's a protocol. Anyone can build on it, anyone can run a relay, and no one controls the network."
