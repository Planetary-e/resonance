Real-World Use Cases

Resonance works wherever people need to find each other without exposing their data to a platform. From freelancing to emergency response, the protocol adapts to the problem — not the other way around.

Scenarios

Where Resonance Fits

Each use case follows the same pattern: describe what you need or offer in plain language, and the protocol finds the complementary match without revealing your identity to anyone but the match itself.

💼

Freelancers & Skills

Find collaborators, clients, or mentors by describing what you do and what you need — without broadcasting your availability on a public marketplace. Your skills stay private until there's a mutual match.

Example

Need: "Looking for a React developer experienced with WebRTC for a 2-week project"
Offer: "Senior frontend engineer, 5 years React, built real-time video apps"

🏠

Housing & Accommodation

Match tenants with landlords, flatmates with each other, or house-sitters with homeowners. Describe your ideal living situation and let the network find the complement — no scraped profiles, no public listings.

Example

Need: "Looking for a quiet room in central Barcelona, 500-700 EUR, available April"
Offer: "Room available in Eixample, 600 EUR/month, quiet flatmate preferred"

🔧

Local Services

Connect with plumbers, electricians, tutors, pet-sitters, or any local service provider. No review gaming, no platform fees, no data harvesting. Just a direct match between someone who needs help and someone who provides it.

Example

Need: "Need someone to fix a leaking kitchen faucet in Gràcia this week"
Offer: "Licensed plumber in Barcelona, available weekdays, fair rates"

🎓

Education & Learning

Find study partners, language exchange buddies, or subject-matter experts. Resonance matches learners with teachers and peers with complementary knowledge — without requiring anyone to join a platform.

Example

Need: "Want to practice conversational Spanish, I speak fluent English"
Offer: "Native Spanish speaker looking for English conversation practice"

🤝

Community Building

Organize around shared interests without creating yet another group chat or forum. Find people in your neighborhood who share a hobby, a cause, or a goal — privately and without a centralized directory.

Example

Need: "Looking for people to start a community garden in Poblenou"
Offer: "Experienced urban gardener, have seeds and tools, looking for a plot and partners"

🚨

Emergency Response

During crises, match people who need help with those who can provide it — shelter, transport, medical supplies, translation. No registration, no app downloads, no data collection during the most vulnerable moments.

Example

Need: "Family of 4 needs temporary shelter near Valencia after flooding"
Offer: "Have spare bedrooms and supplies, can host a family short-term"

Comparison

What Makes Resonance Different

Centralized platforms solve matching by collecting all the data in one place. Resonance proves that's unnecessary.

Centralized Platforms Planetary Resonance
Data storage Platform holds all user data Data stays on your device; relays see only noisy vectors
Identity Email, phone, social login required did:key (Ed25519) generated locally — no PII needed
Matching Keyword search, filters, algorithms Semantic complementary matching via 768-dim embeddings
Privacy Trust the platform's policy Differential privacy (ε-calibrated Laplace noise) — mathematical guarantee
Communication In-app messages the platform can read E2E encrypted channels (X25519 DH + XSalsa20-Poly1305)
Disclosure Full profile visible to everyone Progressive: category → detail → contact, on your terms
Resilience Single point of failure Decentralized relay mesh; any node can go down
Cost Platform fees, subscriptions, ads Free, open source, MIT licensed
The Core Difference

Centralized platforms need your data to work. Resonance is designed so that no one — not even the relay operators — can reconstruct what you said. The matching still works because similarity is preserved in the embedding space, even after noise perturbation (82-86% recall at ε=1.0).
