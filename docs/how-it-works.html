How It Works

Five steps from thought to connection. No accounts, no tracking, no middleman reading your data. Just math, privacy, and human intent.

✎ 1

Describe

Write what you need or offer in plain language. No forms, no categories, no dropdowns. Just say it like you'd say it to a friend.

"I need a plumber in Barcelona"
"Frontend developer available for freelance work"
"Looking for a co-founder for an open-source climate project"

The system understands natural language. It doesn't need you to fit your intent into a taxonomy someone else designed. You describe your reality, and the network finds the complement.

🧠 2

Embed

Your device converts your text into a 768-dimensional mathematical vector using an AI model (Nomic-embed-text) running entirely on your machine. This captures the semantic meaning of your text — not keywords, but the underlying intent and context.

"I need a plumber in Barcelona"
  ↓ local AI model ↓
[0.0234, -0.1121, 0.0873, 0.0412, -0.0558, ... ] × 768 dimensions

Nothing is sent anywhere yet. The model runs locally, in a single forward pass, typically in under 100ms on modern hardware. Your words stay on your device. Only the mathematical representation will travel.

🛡 3

Protect

Before sending anything to the network, your device adds calibrated noise to the vector. This is differential privacy — like whispering through static. The relay can find approximate matches, but cannot reconstruct what you actually wrote.

original: [0.0234, -0.1121, 0.0873, ...]
   + noise: [0.0081, -0.0344, 0.0192, ...] ← Laplace(0, 1/ε)
  = noisy: [0.0315, -0.1465, 0.1065, ...]

You choose your privacy level. More noise means more privacy but fewer matches. Less noise means better matching but a slightly more visible signal.

Low Almost no noise. Best matching accuracy. Suitable when the content isn't sensitive.
Medium Balanced privacy and utility. The default. Good for most use cases.
High Heavy noise. Maximum privacy. Fewer matches, but the relay sees almost nothing.
🔗 4

Match

The noisy vector goes to relay servers that maintain an index of all published vectors. When a new need arrives, the relay searches only the offers index — and vice versa. This asymmetry is intentional: needs find offers, offers find needs.

incoming need vector → search offers index
incoming offer vector → search needs index
similarity > threshold? → notify both parties

If a complementary match is found above the similarity threshold, both parties receive a notification. The relay never sees the original text — it operates purely on noisy vectors and cosine similarity scores.

🤝 5

Connect

You and your match open a direct encrypted channel — no relay involved. You exchange your true, unperturbed embeddings to confirm the match is real. If confirmed, you can progressively share details: category first, then specifics, then contact info.

1. Match notification (similarity score only)
2. Mutual consent to open channel
3. Exchange true embeddings → confirm match
4. Progressive disclosure: category → details → contact

You control every step. No information is shared without your explicit action. The channel uses X25519 Diffie-Hellman key exchange and XSalsa20-Poly1305 authenticated encryption. Not even the relay operator can read your messages.
