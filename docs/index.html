Key Insight

The relay network never sees your original text. It operates on noisy vector embeddings that are close enough to find meaningful matches but too lossy to reconstruct what you actually said.

Privacy by Design

What Makes It Different

Every matching platform today requires you to hand your data to a middleman. Resonance is built on a different assumption: your data is yours.

🛡

No Middleman

Your data stays on your device. The network only sees mathematical noise — vector embeddings perturbed with differential privacy. No server ever holds your raw descriptions, preferences, or identity.

🔀

Complementary Matching

Resonance matches needs to offers, not duplicates to duplicates. When you say "I need a plumber," the system finds someone who says "I do plumbing" — it understands intent and reciprocity.

🔓

Progressive Disclosure

You decide what to share, when, and with whom. A match is just the start — you reveal details gradually through an encrypted channel, on your terms. No profile pages. No public data.

Methodology

Benchmarks measured using all-MiniLM-L6-v2 embeddings with epsilon-calibrated Laplacian noise on a consumer device. Recall is measured as the proportion of true top-10 matches preserved after privacy perturbation. Full evaluation reports are available in the evaluation results.
