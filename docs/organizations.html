For Organizations

Resonance for Organizations

Deploy privacy-preserving matching for your team, community, or consortium. Internal skills discovery, member coordination, and partner matching — with zero data liability and no central database to breach.

Why Resonance

The Problem with Internal Matching

Organizations need to connect people — employees with projects, members with mentors, partners with opportunities. Today, this requires centralized databases that create liability, siloed platforms that fragment communication, and HR tools that employees don't trust with honest self-assessment.

🔍

Internal Skills Matching

Help employees find collaborators across teams and departments. "I need someone who understands Kubernetes networking" matches with "I've been running production K8s clusters for 3 years" — without publishing a skills directory.

🤝

Community Matching

Connect members of a professional association, alumni network, or cooperative. Facilitate introductions between people with complementary interests without maintaining a profile database.

🛡

No Data Liability

Your relay never stores raw descriptions, personal data, or identity information. If it's compromised, attackers get noisy vectors — mathematically useless for reconstructing the original text. No PII means no breach notification, no GDPR fines.

Deployment

Deployment Options

Resonance is flexible enough to fit your organization's infrastructure and security requirements.

Recommended

Self-Hosted Relay

Run a relay on your own infrastructure — a single server, a VM, or a container. Your relay serves only your organization's members, giving you control over the matching network while maintaining the protocol's privacy guarantees.

# Deploy on your infrastructure resonance init --relay resonance serve --relay --port 4444
Alternative

Community Relay

Join an existing public relay mesh. Your members' data is still protected by differential privacy — the relay only sees noisy vectors. This option requires zero infrastructure but shares the relay with other organizations and individuals.

# Connect to a community relay resonance init --relay wss://relay.example.com

For IT Teams

The relay is a single Node.js process. It stores only differentially-private vectors in memory (no database required for basic deployments). Resource usage is minimal: a small VM or container is sufficient for organizations of any size. The relay exposes a WebSocket endpoint that members connect to via the CLI.

Compliance

Privacy & Compliance Benefits

Resonance doesn't just minimize data collection — it eliminates it architecturally. This creates compliance advantages that are impossible with traditional centralized tools.

🔒

No PII Stored Centrally

The relay never receives names, emails, descriptions, or any personally identifiable information. Members' raw text is embedded and noise-perturbed on their own devices before anything leaves their machine.

🇪🇺

GDPR-Friendly by Architecture

No personal data processing means no consent forms, no data processing agreements, no right-to-erasure obligations on the relay side. The architecture makes most GDPR obligations moot because the data never exists centrally.

💥

No Data Breaches Possible

If your relay is compromised, the attacker gets noisy 768-dimensional vectors. Reconstructing the original text from a Laplace-perturbed embedding is computationally infeasible — that's a mathematical guarantee, not a policy promise.

🔐

Employee Trust

Employees can honestly describe their skills, interests, and career goals because they know the organization can't see their raw input. This leads to better matching and more honest self-assessment.

🔍

Auditable

The entire protocol is open source (MIT licensed). Your security team can audit every line of code, verify the privacy guarantees, and confirm that the relay truly cannot reconstruct member data.

🌍

Cross-Border Ready

No personal data crosses borders because no personal data exists on the network. Noisy vectors are not personal data under any reasonable interpretation of privacy law.

Quickstart

Deploy a Relay in 5 Minutes

Get your organization's relay running and invite members to start matching.

1. Install Resonance

npm install -g @planetary/resonance

2. Initialize a Relay Node

resonance init --relay

3. Start the Relay

resonance serve --relay --port 4444

4. Share the Relay URL with Members

Distribute your relay's WebSocket URL to team members. They connect with:

# Each member runs: resonance init --relay wss://your-relay.example.com:4444 resonance publish --type offer "Describe your skills and what you can help with" resonance publish --type need "Describe what you're looking for"
That's It

Matches happen automatically. When two members have complementary needs and offers above the relay threshold (0.50), they're notified and can open an encrypted channel to discuss details. The relay never knows what they said.
